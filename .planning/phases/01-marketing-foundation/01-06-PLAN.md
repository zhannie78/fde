<task type="checkpoint:decision" gate="blocking">
  <decision>DNS ownership for the production domain: Cloudflare-hosted DNS vs Netlify DNS delegation</decision>
  <context>
    Per RESEARCH Pitfall 6, Cloudflare Email Routing (the free founder-email path in D-05) REQUIRES the domain's DNS to be hosted at Cloudflare. If DNS is delegated to Netlify instead, Cloudflare Email Routing becomes unavailable without a later disruptive nameserver change. This decision must be made ONCE, now, before the domain is connected — it is mutually exclusive and hard to reverse cleanly later.
  </context>
  <options>
    <option id="cloudflare-dns">
      <name>Keep DNS at Cloudflare, point records at Netlify</name>
      <pros>Free Cloudflare Email Routing for the matching-domain founder email (D-05); apex CNAME-flattening supported.</pros>
      <cons>Slightly more manual DNS record setup (A/CNAME to Netlify's load balancer + www CNAME); two dashboards.</cons>
    </option>
    <option id="netlify-dns">
      <name>Delegate DNS to Netlify</name>
      <pros>Simplest custom-domain setup; one dashboard.</pros>
      <cons>Cloudflare Email Routing unavailable — founder email must come from registrar-level forwarding instead; switching later causes a brief outage.</cons>
    </option>
  </options>
  <resume-signal>Select: cloudflare-dns or netlify-dns (and confirm the corresponding free email-forwarding path for D-05).</resume-signal>
